Problems Parents Have with In-Person Classes for NYC Public Schools

by Juliet Jeske

October 10, 2020


The parents of 1,126,501 New York City public school students are nervous about sending their children to in-person hybrid classes during the COVID-19 pandemic. The Department of Education has been trying to assuage their fears, but major concerns remain. About 46% of parents have opted for remote-only learning. Many parents feel they haven't been given a voice.


Schools don't have enough safety monitors, school nurses or PPE for teachers and staff. There isn't enough personnel to take daily temperatures. Most school buildings are old and won't have adequate ventilation systems in place. Source

Hybrid learning does not allow parents to return to work five days a week. Children are placed in a small cohort of nine students and one teacher with rotating schedules that change every week. Parents won't be able to adapt their work schedules to the school schedule. Source

Remote learning classes and in-person classes won't be taught by the same teachers. Some teachers have quit or have opted to teach remotely. About 21% of teachers took a medical exemption. In some cases, schools need twice as many teachers as they would in normal circumstances. Source

Some electives are now taught remote only. For some classes students do not have a live instructor. Children are basically teaching themselves from online courses. Many parents have opted for outside tutoring leaving the financially disadvantaged kids even further behind. Source

Sending children back to in-person classes will spread COVID-19. Students could infect each other, teachers, staff or family members. In-person classes will be shut down if the positivity rate for COVID-19 reaches 3%. Source
